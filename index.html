Automating the State: Promise, Peril, and the Politics of AI in Public Administration

This paper examines the integration of artificial intelligence into public administration — from welfare algorithms to automated decision-making systems — analysing accountability deficits, the erosion of due process, and the political economy of digital state transformation. Drawing on case studies including the Netherlands Kinderopvangtoeslag scandal and Australia's Robodebt scheme, it argues that citizens can vote out politicians, but they cannot vote out an algorithm, and develops a governance framework for rights-respecting automation in democratic states.

Author

Year

Area

Abstract

Governments around the world are turning to artificial intelligence to reform how they deliver services, make decisions, and govern populations. This paper offers a systematic analysis of AI integration in public administration — examining both the transformative potential and the structural risks of algorithmic governance. It draws on international case studies, including the Netherlands Kinderopvangtoeslag (Child Benefit) scandal and Australia's Robodebt scheme, to demonstrate how automated decision-making can systematically harm citizens when accountability mechanisms fail to keep pace with technological change. The paper analyses the political economy of AI adoption in public institutions, arguing that opacity, speed, and scale create accountability vacuums incompatible with democratic governance. It concludes by proposing an integrated framework — covering transparency requirements, human oversight mandates, and redress mechanisms — for rights-respecting AI in democratic states.

About SAMANVAYA

समन्वय Samanvaya — Sanskrit

Meaning synthesis, coordination, or bringing together — the integration of diverse ideas into coherent understanding. Applied here: the synthesis of technology, governance, and public purpose.

The Initiative

Samanvaya is an independent research initiative dedicated to studying the intersection of artificial intelligence, governance, and public policy. The initiative focuses on how emerging technologies reshape state capacity, public administration, and democratic institutions.

We produce rigorous, evidence-based research that bridges academic scholarship and practical policy. Our work is oriented toward India and South Asia, but engages with comparative international governance and regulatory developments.

Samanvaya is non-partisan and does not accept funding that would compromise the independence of its research findings or editorial positions.

Why This Research Matters

Governments worldwide are adopting AI systems at speed — for welfare delivery, law enforcement, immigration, taxation, and public health. These decisions have profound consequences for citizens, institutions, and the future of democratic accountability.

Yet the policy frameworks, legal instruments, and institutional capacities needed to govern AI in the public sector remain underdeveloped. Samanvaya exists to help close that gap through independent, rigorous research.
