What We Study

The politics of the automated state

Governments are turning to AI to reform how they deliver services, make decisions, and govern populations. These decisions carry profound consequences for citizens, institutions, and democratic accountability — yet the policy frameworks needed to govern AI in the public sector remain dangerously underdeveloped.

SVADHINA exists to close that gap. We produce rigorous, evidence-based research that is independent, non-partisan, and India-focused while engaging with comparative international governance.

Working Paper No. 1

Automating the State: Promise, Peril, and the Politics of AI in Public Administration

AuthorSwastik Sharma
Year2026
SeriesSVADHINA Working Papers
AreaAI Governance · Public Administration
Abstract

Governments around the world are turning to artificial intelligence to reform how they deliver services, make decisions, and govern populations. This paper offers a systematic analysis of AI integration in public administration — examining both the transformative potential and the structural risks of algorithmic governance. Drawing on case studies including the Netherlands Kinderopvangtoeslag (Child Benefit) scandal and Australia's Robodebt scheme, it demonstrates how automated decision-making can systematically harm citizens when accountability mechanisms fail to keep pace with technological change.

“Citizens can vote out politicians — they cannot vote out an algorithm.”

About

About SVADHINA

स्वाधीना Svadhīnā — Sanskrit · sva (self) + adhīna (under authority of)

Meaning self-governed, free, or autonomous — one who is under their own authority. Applied here: the pursuit of self-determining, rights-grounded governance in the age of artificial intelligence.

The Initiative

SVADHINA is an independent research initiative dedicated to studying the intersection of artificial intelligence, governance, and public policy. We focus on how emerging technologies reshape state capacity, public administration, and democratic institutions.

We produce rigorous, evidence-based research bridging academic scholarship and practical policy. Our work is oriented toward India and South Asia while engaging with comparative international governance.

SVADHINA is non-partisan and maintains full editorial independence. We do not accept funding that would compromise the independence of our research.

Why This Research Matters

Governments worldwide are adopting AI systems at speed — for welfare delivery, law enforcement, immigration, taxation, and public health. Yet the policy frameworks needed to govern AI in the public sector remain underdeveloped. SVADHINA exists to help close that gap.
